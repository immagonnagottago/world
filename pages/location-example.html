Thornwall

A fortified trade city perched on the edge of the Ashen Plateau, where three rival merchant houses have held an uneasy peace for two centuries.

Thornwall sits where the Merchant's Road meets the edge of the Ashen Plateau — a natural chokepoint that has made it simultaneously wealthy and contested for six centuries. Its defining feature is the Spikewall: a 40-foot perimeter of black ironwood posts driven into the plateau's edge, each carved with the sigils of the city's founders.

Overview

The city is divided into three districts, one controlled by each of the great merchant houses: House Draveth (textiles and dyes), House Osrik (ironwork and weapons), and House Cael (information and coin). Each district has its own character — Draveth's quarter is colorful and chaotic, Osrik's is sooty and industrial, Cael's is quiet, clean, and watched.

The Triune Council governs jointly, with each house holding one seat. Decisions require two votes to pass, which means any two houses can block the third. This has produced two centuries of functional (if grudging) stability.

History

Thornwall was founded as a waystation by the Merchant's Compact in Year 412 of the Third Age, after repeated bandit raids on the unprotected road. The three founding families each contributed one third of the construction costs, and in doing so locked themselves into a permanent three-way ownership dispute that has never been resolved.

The city survived the Collapse of 609 when most northern settlements fell — its walls held because all three houses agreed, for once, that letting them fail would be worse than cooperating. This shared survival is the foundation of the Triune Compact still in force today.

"In Thornwall, every smile is a negotiation and every handshake is a binding contract. Learn which is which before you arrive, or you'll leave wearing neither your boots nor your dignity."

— Maret Solven, A Traveler's Guide to the Northern Reach

Notable Places

  • The Ironroot Market — A vast open-air bazaar operating every day regardless of weather, where anything legal (and much that isn't) can be bought if you know the right stalls.
  • The Spikewall — The city's famous outer perimeter, also used as an execution ground and, more commonly, as a place to post public notices.
  • The Weighing Hall — Neutral ground maintained by all three houses, where contracts are signed and debts acknowledged. Violation of Weighing Hall agreements is the one offense all three houses agree to punish.
  • The Gray Steps — A slum district that technically belongs to no house, built into the plateau's edge. Densely populated and poorly mapped.

Relations & Connections

Allies

Thornwall maintains trade relationships with most major settlements in the Northern Reach, and holds a formal neutrality agreement with the Iron Throne to its south.

Tensions

The three merchant houses are in constant low-level conflict with each other. Currently, House Draveth is suspected of bribing Ashen Plateau raiders to attack Osrik supply caravans — though nothing has been proven in the Weighing Hall.

Adventure Hooks

A sealed vault in the Weighing Hall has not been opened since Year 612. House Cael holds one key, House Osrik holds the second — but the third key's location is unknown. All three houses quietly want it found before the others do.
